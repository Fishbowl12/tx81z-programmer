LFO Controls

The LFO (low frequency oscillator) is used to create cyclical changes in the sound, like vibrato and tremolo. This section determines the character of the LFO, but the LFO won't have any effect until you assign it some depth in the pitch modulation or amplitude modulation sections.

LFO Waveform

The waveform determines the "shape" of the cyclical change in the sound. There are four waveforms: saw up, square, triangle, and sample and hold. The triangle wave is the most conventional waveform, and can be used for ordinary vibrato and tremolo effects. The other waveforms can be used for interesting special effects.

Key Sync

The LFO can cycle continuously or its waveform can be reset each time you press a key and this is what the key sync does.

Speed

Speed determines the frequency of the LFO. I don't know the exact formula for the relationship between the speed setting and the frequency. From what I gather the ratio was arbitrarily chosen.

Delay

Delay determines how long it takes for the LFO to fade in when you press a key.
